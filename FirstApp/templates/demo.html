About SUTD System

Introduction

In today's fast-paced world, technology serves as a powerful instrument for social betterment. Many households contain unused items—clothes, books, utensils, furniture, or electronic gadgets—lying neglected. Concurrently, underprivileged communities face severe daily struggles without access to basic necessities.

Share of Unused Things at Doorstep (SUTD), under the banner of Share & Care, bridges the gap between waste and need. We provide a structured, transparent, and digital channel to collect these idle materials directly from donors' homes and redistribute them to the right beneficiaries.

Core Objectives

🤝

Social Bridge

Connecting generous donors directly with verified regional beneficiaries and local shelter homes.

♻️

Eco Sustainability

Reducing household waste and promoting environmental sustainability by giving useful things a second life.

📊

Transparent Tracking

Maintaining clear records of collection, stock checks, and deliveries to ensure integrity.

How It Works

The platform runs on a streamlined 3-step operational workflow:

1. Register & List: Donors sign up and list their items by category (Electronics, Clothing, Furniture, Books) along with status and batch details.

2. Collection & Inspection: Verified working members schedule doorstep pick-ups and log incoming collection items into the central stock database.

3. Redistribution: Administrators and volunteers coordinate distribution schedules to dispatch materials directly to regional underprivileged families and partner NGOs.
